# Google AI Mode SEO: What Website Owners Should Do

Google AI Mode does not require a separate secret ranking system. Google says the same SEO fundamentals still apply: pages need to be crawlable, indexable, helpful, and eligible to appear in Search with a snippet. What changes is the shape of the result: AI Mode can use query fan-out to explore a topic through multiple related searches, so pages that answer one narrow angle well may appear inside a broader AI response.

## What Google says about AI Mode

Google's official AI features documentation says AI Overviews and AI Mode surface supporting links and that there are no additional technical requirements beyond normal Search eligibility. Google also explains that AI Mode may use query fan-out, issuing multiple related searches across subtopics before building a response.

## AI Mode vs classic SEO

| Area | Classic Search | AI Mode |
|---|---|---|
| Query shape | Often one query | May expand into related subqueries |
| Result type | Ranked links | Synthesized answer plus supporting links |
| Page opportunity | Best overall page | Best page for one useful sub-answer |
| Content need | Relevance and quality | Relevance, quality, and extractable clarity |
| Measurement | Rankings, clicks, impressions | Search Console totals plus downstream engagement |

The practical implication is simple: broad pillar pages still matter, but supporting pages matter more than many teams expect. A comparison page, checklist, FAQ, or implementation guide can become useful if AI Mode fans out into that exact subproblem.

## What query fan-out changes

Query fan-out rewards topical coverage without rewarding filler. If a user asks, "How do I prepare an ecommerce site for AI shopping agents?", a system may need separate evidence for product data, crawler access, checkout, merchant feeds, and returns.

That means your site benefits from:

- one clear pillar page
- narrower pages for distinct sub-questions
- consistent entity naming
- strong internal linking
- evidence-rich answers that can stand alone

## What to improve on the page

| Element | Good practice for AI Mode |
|---|---|
| Title | Match the real query, not a vague brand phrase |
| Intro | Answer the question in the first 60 to 80 words |
| H2s | Break the topic into actual sub-questions |
| Tables | Compare options, criteria, or tradeoffs clearly |
| Sources | Link to official or primary references |
| Internal links | Connect related subtopics, not random pages |
| Freshness | Update when standards, pricing, or protocol status changes |

Do not rewrite pages into robotic summary blocks. AI Mode still depends on useful pages for people. The best extraction-friendly pages are often the best human pages too.

## Search Console and measurement

Google documentation updates note that AI Mode counts toward Search Console totals. That does not create a separate "AI Mode rankings" report for every site owner, but it does mean AI-driven exposure can affect broader performance trends.

Track:

- impressions for informational pages
- click-through rate by query class
- growth of longtail pages inside a cluster
- conversions from pages that earn support-link traffic
- engagement after the click

The [Bing AI Performance report guide](/docs/bing-ai-performance-report/) is useful alongside Google Search Console because it gives another view of AI citations and cited URLs.

## What not to do

Avoid five common mistakes:

1. Creating shallow "AI Mode" pages with no new information.
2. Publishing ten pages that answer the same intent with slightly different wording.
3. Hiding important facts inside images or interactive widgets only.
4. Treating AI Mode as a replacement for crawlability, indexing, or site architecture.
5. Optimizing only for mentions instead of useful next actions.

AI visibility is the read layer. If the site also needs to be used by agents, connect it to the [execution layer](/docs/execution-layer/).

## FAQ

### Is there special schema for Google AI Mode?

Google says there are no additional technical requirements beyond normal Search eligibility. Use structured data when it helps represent the visible content correctly.

### Is AI Mode different from AI Overviews?

Yes. Google says they may use different models and techniques. AI Mode is especially useful for complex exploration and comparisons.

### Does AI Mode make long articles rank better?

Not automatically. Useful coverage matters more than raw length. A concise comparison page can be more useful than a bloated guide.

### What should I update first?

Fix crawlability, write a direct answer-first intro, and build supporting pages for distinct sub-questions around your strongest topics.

## Bottom line

Google AI Mode SEO is still SEO, but the site architecture matters more. Build pages that answer distinct user needs, connect them well, and keep the information current enough that an AI system can trust the page as a supporting source.
