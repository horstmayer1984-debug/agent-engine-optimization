Most agencies that advertise AEO services in 2026 are selling repackaged GEO: schema markup, answer-first content, and AI visibility monitoring. That work matters. But it only covers the read layer. If you need an agency that handles the execution layer (APIs, protocols, deterministic transaction endpoints), the field narrows dramatically.

Here is how to evaluate AEO agencies for ecommerce and what to expect from each service tier.

## What real AEO expertise looks like

An AEO agency that understands Agent Engine Optimization, not just Answer Engine Optimization, should be able to discuss these topics concretely: the difference between read layer and [execution layer](/docs/execution-layer/) optimization, protocol implementation (MCP, UCP, ACP), deterministic endpoint design, [agent decision tree](/docs/agent-decision-trees/) pass/fail criteria, and how to measure agent-driven conversions separately from human traffic.

If the agency's pitch focuses exclusively on "getting cited in ChatGPT" and "optimizing for AI Overviews," they are selling GEO under the AEO label. Useful work, but not the full picture.

## Three tiers of AEO services

### Tier 1: Read layer optimization (most agencies)

What they do: content restructuring for AI extraction, schema markup implementation, llms.txt creation, FAQ optimization, AI citation monitoring, and competitor visibility analysis.

Cost: typically 2,000 to 8,000 euros per month.

This is where most agencies operate in April 2026. The work is valuable and produces measurable results (AI citation rates, referral traffic) within 4 to 8 weeks. Most ecommerce brands should start here.

Platforms like [HubSpot AEO and Webflow AEO](/docs/hubspot-webflow-aeo-launch/) are beginning to automate parts of this tier, which will put pressure on agencies that offer only read-layer services.

### Tier 2: Read layer plus technical AEO

What they add: structured data architecture across the full product catalog, real-time product feed optimization for agent consumption, API documentation review, endpoint design consulting, and [agent readiness audits](/docs/audit/) that cover both layers.

Cost: typically 5,000 to 15,000 euros per month.

Fewer agencies operate at this level. The work requires technical staff who understand API design, not just content strategy. Look for agencies with engineering capability, not just marketing teams.

### Tier 3: Full execution layer implementation

What they add: MCP server development, UCP Capability Declaration implementation, deterministic checkout endpoint design, [x402 micropayment](/docs/x402-agent-payments/) integration, agent feedback loop architecture, and protocol compliance testing.

Cost: typically 10,000 to 30,000 euros per month or project-based pricing from 25,000 euros.

Very few agencies offer this in April 2026. Most execution layer work is done by in-house engineering teams or specialized consultancies rather than traditional marketing agencies.

## How to evaluate an AEO agency

### Questions to ask

Can you explain the difference between Answer Engine Optimization and Agent Engine Optimization? If they conflate the two, they offer only read-layer services.

What protocols have you implemented? MCP, UCP, ACP, A2A. If they have not implemented any, their AEO expertise is theoretical.

How do you measure agent-specific outcomes? If they only track AI citations and not endpoint completion rates, agent conversion rates, or agent retention, they are measuring the read layer only.

Can you show a case study where an agent completed a transaction through infrastructure you built? This separates agencies with execution layer experience from those with content-only expertise.

### Red flags

Promising "page 1 rankings in AI answers" with guaranteed timelines. AI citation is not as controllable as traditional SEO ranking.

Offering AEO as an add-on to existing SEO retainers without additional technical staff. Real AEO requires different skills.

No distinction between Answer Engine Optimization and Agent Engine Optimization in their service descriptions.

Using "AEO" and "GEO" interchangeably. They are related but different disciplines with different technical requirements.

### Green flags

Separate service tiers for read layer and execution layer. Engineers on the team, not just content strategists. Published case studies with agent-specific metrics. Familiarity with current protocols (MCP, UCP, ACP). Understanding of [deterministic decision trees](/docs/agent-decision-trees/) and how agents evaluate sites.

## Building in-house vs hiring an agency

For Tier 1 (read layer), agencies deliver faster results because they have established workflows and tools. The investment is moderate and the ROI is measurable within weeks.

For Tier 2 and 3 (technical and execution layer), building in-house capability often makes more sense because: the work is deeply integrated with your product and engineering teams, the knowledge compounds internally, and the competitive advantage is stronger when it is proprietary.

A common pattern: hire an agency for Tier 1, use the [AEO implementation guide](/docs/implement-aeo/) for Tier 2 work internally, and build Tier 3 capability with your engineering team using the [developer checklist](/docs/agent-ready-web-apps/).

## FAQ

**Are there specialized AEO agencies for ecommerce?**
In April 2026, most agencies offering AEO services are traditional SEO or digital marketing agencies that have added AEO to their service list. Specialized AEO-only agencies are emerging but still rare. Evaluate based on capability, not branding.

**How much should I budget for AEO agency services?**
Start with 2,000 to 5,000 euros per month for read-layer optimization. Scale to 10,000 or more per month if you need execution layer work. Project-based audits start at 2,500 euros.

**Can my existing SEO agency handle AEO?**
For Tier 1 (read layer), likely yes with some upskilling. For Tier 2 and 3, probably not unless they have engineering staff. Ask the evaluation questions above to assess their readiness.

**Should I wait for more AEO agencies to emerge?**
No. The read layer work can start immediately with existing agencies or in-house. Waiting means competitors build citation patterns that become harder to displace. The execution layer work should start in parallel with your engineering team.

**What is the ROI of hiring an AEO agency?**
Read-layer optimization typically shows AI citation increases within 4 to 8 weeks. AI-referred traffic converts at higher rates than traditional organic. Exact ROI depends on your industry and conversion value.
