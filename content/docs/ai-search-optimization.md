AI Search Optimization is the practice of making a website discoverable, understandable, citeable and usable across AI-powered discovery systems. It includes SEO, Answer Engine Optimization, Generative Engine Optimization and Agent Engine Optimization.

The phrase "AI search" is used loosely. Some people mean Google AI Overviews. Others mean ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, Claude or AI assistants inside browsers and apps. The common shift is clear: users increasingly expect answers, summaries, comparisons and actions rather than simple result lists.

## The AI Optimization Stack

A useful way to understand AI Search Optimization is the AI Optimization Stack:

SEO: Can search engines find and index the page? [Answer Engine Optimization](/docs/answer-engine-optimization/): Can AI systems extract a clear answer? [GEO](/docs/generative-engine-optimization/): Can generative engines cite or mention the source? [Agent Engine Optimization](/docs/what-is-aeo/): Can autonomous agents use the site to complete a task?

Each layer depends on the one below it. A site that cannot be crawled will struggle with AI visibility. A site that is visible but unclear will struggle to be cited. A site that is cited but not actionable will struggle in agent-led journeys.

## SEO remains the foundation

AI Search Optimization does not replace technical SEO. Crawlability, indexability, internal linking, canonical URLs, page speed, structured data and useful content still matter.

The difference is that content now has to serve more than one interface. It must work for search result pages, answer systems, generated summaries and agent workflows.

## From answers to actions

AI Search Optimization should not stop at answer visibility. The next shift is from answers to actions. When users trust AI systems with tasks, they will ask agents to compare, book, buy, request, update and manage. That changes the website's role. A website is no longer just a destination. It becomes infrastructure.

## What an AI-optimized site should include

A strong AI Search Optimization strategy includes: technical SEO hygiene, clear information architecture, topic clusters around real search intent, entity-rich content, FAQ and comparison content, structured data, llms.txt or similar discovery aids, transparent policies, clean forms and conversion paths, and machine-readable product, service or capability data.

The [AI Optimization Maturity Model](/docs/ai-optimization-maturity-model/) provides a level-by-level assessment framework.

## Measure each discovery surface separately

AI search is not one channel. A page can perform well in classic search, appear in generated answers, and still fail when an agent tries to use its data. Build a measurement view that separates those outcomes.

| Layer | Primary question | Useful evidence |
|---|---|---|
| Search | Can users find the page? | Impressions, rankings, clicks, indexed status |
| Answer | Is the page represented accurately? | Citations, mentions, extracted answer quality |
| Agent | Can a task be completed? | Tool calls, validation errors, completion rate |
| Business | Did the interaction create value? | Qualified leads, orders, retained users |

Do not combine these measures into one score until the underlying events are reliable. A rise in citations can hide a broken booking path, while a low click count may be normal when an answer satisfies an informational query.

## FAQ

**Is AI Search Optimization the same as SEO?**
No. SEO is the foundation, but AI Search Optimization also includes answer visibility, generated citations and agent usability.

**What is the first step?**
Fix crawlability and content clarity before chasing advanced AI visibility.

**What is the final layer?**
Agent Engine Optimization, where websites become actionable for autonomous AI agents.
